# Databricks

Databricks, Inc. is an American enterprise software company founded by the creators of Apache Spark. The company is headquartered in San Francisco, California, and provides a unified analytics platform.

## History

Databricks was founded in 2013 by Ali Ghodsi, Matei Zaharia, Arsalan Tavakoli-Shiraji, Patrick Wendell, Reynold Xin, and Andy Konwinski. The founders were researchers at UC Berkeley's AMPLab who created Apache Spark.

## Leadership

- Ali Ghodsi, Chief Executive Officer and co-founder
- Matei Zaharia, Chief Technologist and co-founder (creator of Apache Spark)
- Reynold Xin, co-founder

## Platform

Databricks offers a lakehouse platform that combines data lakes and data warehouses:

### Key Components
- Delta Lake (open-source storage layer)
- Unity Catalog (data governance)
- Databricks SQL (data warehousing)
- MLflow (machine learning lifecycle)
- Mosaic AI (generative AI capabilities)

## Open Source Contributions

Databricks has contributed significantly to open source:

- Apache Spark (unified analytics engine)
- Delta Lake (storage layer)
- MLflow (ML platform)
- Koalas (pandas API on Spark)

## Competition

Databricks competes with Snowflake in data warehousing and with various cloud providers' native analytics services. The company also competes with Palantir and traditional BI vendors.

## Funding

Databricks has raised over $3.5 billion in funding and was valued at $43 billion in its 2021 funding round. Investors include Andreessen Horowitz, Microsoft, and Amazon.
