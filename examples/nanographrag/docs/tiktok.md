# TikTok

TikTok is a short-form video hosting service owned by Chinese company ByteDance. The platform allows users to create, share, and discover short videos, typically set to music or audio clips.

## History

TikTok was launched in September 2016 by ByteDance for markets outside of China (the Chinese version is called Douyin). ByteDance acquired Musical.ly in November 2017 and merged it with TikTok in August 2018.

## Parent Company

ByteDance is a Chinese technology company founded by Zhang Yiming in 2012 in Beijing. ByteDance also operates:

- Douyin (Chinese TikTok)
- Toutiao (news aggregator)
- Lark/Feishu (workplace collaboration)
- CapCut (video editing)

## Leadership

- Shou Zi Chew, Chief Executive Officer of TikTok (since 2021)
- Liang Rubo, CEO of ByteDance
- Zhang Yiming, founder of ByteDance (stepped down as CEO in 2021)

## Platform Features

TikTok features:

- Short-form videos (15 seconds to 10 minutes)
- For You page algorithmic feed
- Duets and Stitches (video response features)
- Live streaming
- E-commerce integration (TikTok Shop)

## User Base

TikTok has over 1 billion monthly active users globally, with particularly strong adoption among Gen Z users.

## Competition

TikTok competes with:

- Instagram Reels (Meta)
- YouTube Shorts (Google)
- Snapchat Spotlight
- Triller

## Regulatory Issues

TikTok faces regulatory scrutiny in multiple countries over data privacy and national security concerns:

- Banned on government devices in the US, UK, Canada, and EU
- Faced potential ban in the United States
- Investigated by various data protection authorities

## Content Moderation

TikTok employs content moderation combining AI systems and human reviewers to enforce community guidelines and local laws.
