# Tim Cook

Timothy Donald Cook is an American business executive who has been the chief executive officer of Apple Inc. since 2011. He previously served as Apple's chief operating officer under co-founder Steve Jobs.

## Early Life

Tim Cook was born on November 1, 1960, in Mobile, Alabama. He earned a Bachelor of Science in industrial engineering from Auburn University and an MBA from Duke University's Fuqua School of Business.

## Career

### Before Apple
Before joining Apple, Cook worked at IBM for 12 years, rising to become director of North American fulfillment. He then briefly worked at Intelligent Electronics and served as Vice President of Corporate Materials at Compaq.

### At Apple
Cook joined Apple in 1998 as Senior Vice President for Worldwide Operations, recruited by Steve Jobs. He was instrumental in streamlining Apple's supply chain and manufacturing operations.

#### Key Roles
- Senior Vice President of Worldwide Operations (1998-2007)
- Chief Operating Officer (2007-2011)
- Chief Executive Officer (2011-present)

### As CEO
Cook became CEO on August 24, 2011, after Steve Jobs resigned due to health issues. Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011.

#### Achievements as CEO
- Grew Apple to become the first U.S. company to reach $3 trillion market cap
- Launched Apple Watch, AirPods, and Apple Silicon
- Expanded Apple Services (App Store, Apple Music, Apple TV+, iCloud)
- Led transition from Intel to Apple's own M-series chips

## Personal Life

Cook publicly came out as gay in October 2014, becoming the first openly gay CEO of a Fortune 500 company. He has been an advocate for privacy, environmental sustainability, and LGBTQ+ rights.

## Philanthropy

Cook has pledged to donate his entire fortune to charitable causes. He supports various educational initiatives and human rights organizations.
