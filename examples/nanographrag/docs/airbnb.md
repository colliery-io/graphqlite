# Airbnb, Inc.

Airbnb, Inc. is an American company operating an online marketplace for short-term lodging primarily for vacation rentals and tourism activities. The company is headquartered in San Francisco, California.

## History

Airbnb was founded in August 2008 by Brian Chesky, Nathan Blecharczyk, and Joe Gebbia. The name is a shortened version of its original name, AirBedandBreakfast.com. The founders initially rented out air mattresses in their San Francisco apartment to conference attendees.

## Leadership

- Brian Chesky, Chief Executive Officer and co-founder
- Nathan Blecharczyk, Chief Strategy Officer and co-founder
- Joe Gebbia, co-founder (stepped back from day-to-day operations)

## Platform

Airbnb's platform allows hosts to list their properties—whether entire homes, apartments, private rooms, or shared spaces—for short-term rental. The company takes a service fee from both hosts and guests.

### Features
- Experiences (tours and activities hosted by locals)
- Online Experiences (virtual activities)
- Airbnb Luxe (luxury properties)
- Airbnb Plus (verified high-quality homes)

## Funding and IPO

Airbnb went public on December 10, 2020, with shares more than doubling on the first day of trading. The company raised $3.5 billion in the IPO. Early investors included Y Combinator, Sequoia Capital, and Andreessen Horowitz.

## Competition

Airbnb competes with:

- VRBO (Expedia)
- Booking.com
- Hotels (Marriott, Hilton, etc.)
- Homestay.com

## Regulatory Challenges

Airbnb has faced regulatory challenges in many cities over:

- Housing affordability impacts
- Hotel industry competition
- Safety and insurance concerns
- Tax collection requirements

## COVID-19 Impact

The pandemic significantly impacted Airbnb's business initially, but the company recovered as travelers sought longer stays and remote work destinations.
