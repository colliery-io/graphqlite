# Cisco Systems, Inc.

Cisco Systems is an American multinational technology company headquartered in San Jose, California. The company is the world's largest networking equipment manufacturer.

## History

Founded in 1984 by Leonard Bosack and Sandy Lerner, a married couple who worked at Stanford University. The name "Cisco" is derived from San Francisco.

## Leadership

- Chuck Robbins, Chairman and CEO (since 2015)
- Previous CEOs: John Chambers (1995-2015), John Morgridge

## Products and Services

### Networking
- Routers and switches
- Wireless solutions
- SD-WAN

### Security
- Firewalls
- Identity services
- Threat intelligence (Talos)

### Collaboration
- Webex (video conferencing)
- Unified communications

### Data Center
- Servers (UCS)
- Storage networking

## Market Position

Cisco dominates the enterprise networking market and is a key infrastructure provider for the internet.

## Acquisitions

### Notable Acquisitions
- WebEx (2007)
- Meraki (2012)
- AppDynamics (2017)
- Duo Security (2018)
- Splunk (2023, $28 billion)

## John Chambers Era

Under CEO John Chambers, Cisco grew from $70 million to $47 billion in revenue and became synonymous with internet infrastructure.

## Certifications

Cisco certifications (CCNA, CCNP, CCIE) are among the most recognized in the IT industry.
