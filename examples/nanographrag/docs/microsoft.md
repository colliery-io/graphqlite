# Microsoft Corporation

Microsoft Corporation is an American multinational technology corporation headquartered in Redmond, Washington. The company was founded by Bill Gates and Paul Allen on April 4, 1975, to develop and sell BASIC interpreters for the Altair 8800.

## Leadership

Satya Nadella has served as Chief Executive Officer since February 2014, succeeding Steve Ballmer. Bill Gates stepped down as chairman in 2014 but remains as technology advisor. Other key executives include:

- Brad Smith, Vice Chair and President
- Amy Hood, Chief Financial Officer
- Judson Althoff, Executive Vice President of Commercial Business

## Products and Services

Microsoft's flagship products include Windows operating system, Microsoft 365 (formerly Office), Azure cloud platform, and Xbox gaming. The company also develops Surface hardware, LinkedIn professional network, and GitHub developer platform.

## Acquisitions

Major acquisitions include:

- LinkedIn (2016) for $26.2 billion
- GitHub (2018) for $7.5 billion
- ZeniMax Media (2021) for $7.5 billion
- Activision Blizzard (2023) for $68.7 billion
- Nuance Communications (2022) for $19.7 billion

## Cloud Computing

Microsoft Azure is the second-largest cloud infrastructure provider behind Amazon Web Services. The company has invested heavily in data centers worldwide and partners with OpenAI to provide AI services through Azure.

## Competition

Microsoft competes with Google in productivity software and cloud services, with Amazon in cloud infrastructure, with Sony and Nintendo in gaming, and with Apple in personal computing and mobile devices.

## AI Investments

Microsoft has invested over $13 billion in OpenAI and integrated ChatGPT technology into Bing search, Microsoft 365 Copilot, and Azure OpenAI Service.
