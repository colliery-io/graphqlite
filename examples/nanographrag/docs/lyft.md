# Lyft, Inc.

Lyft, Inc. is an American company that offers ride-hailing, vehicles for hire, motorized scooters, a bicycle-sharing system, and food delivery. The company is headquartered in San Francisco, California.

## History

Lyft was founded in 2012 by Logan Green and John Zimmer as a service of Zimride, a long-distance carpooling company they had started in 2007. The Lyft brand launched in June 2012. The company went public in March 2019.

## Leadership

- David Risher, Chief Executive Officer (since 2023)
- Logan Green, co-founder and Chairman
- John Zimmer, co-founder and Vice Chairman
- Erin Brewer, Chief Financial Officer

## Services

### Lyft Rideshare
Core ride-hailing service with various options:
- Lyft (standard)
- Lyft XL (larger vehicles)
- Lux (premium)
- Priority (faster pickup)

### Lyft Pink
Subscription service offering ride discounts and perks.

### Bikes and Scooters
Bike-sharing and scooter-sharing in select cities.

### Autonomous Vehicles
Lyft has partnered with companies like Waymo for autonomous vehicle pilots.

## Driver Classification

Like Uber, Lyft has faced legal challenges regarding whether drivers should be classified as employees or independent contractors.

## Competition

Lyft's primary competitor is Uber, which holds a larger market share in the United States. Lyft focuses exclusively on North American markets.

## Strategic Focus

Under CEO David Risher, Lyft has focused on cost reduction, improving driver and rider experience, and competing more effectively with Uber on price and service quality.
