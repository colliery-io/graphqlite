# X Corp. (Twitter)

X Corp. is an American social media company that operates X (formerly Twitter), a microblogging and social networking service. The company is owned by Elon Musk.

## History

Twitter was founded in March 2006 by Jack Dorsey, Noah Glass, Biz Stone, and Evan Williams at Odeo, a podcasting company. The first tweet was sent by Dorsey on March 21, 2006. The company went public in November 2013.

In October 2022, Elon Musk completed his acquisition of Twitter for $44 billion, taking the company private. In 2023, the company rebranded from Twitter to X.

## Leadership

- Elon Musk, Owner and CTO
- Linda Yaccarino, Chief Executive Officer (since 2023)
- Jack Dorsey, co-founder and former CEO (served twice: 2006-2008, 2015-2021)

## Platform Features

### Posts (formerly Tweets)
Short-form posts limited to 280 characters for free users, with longer limits for premium subscribers.

### X Premium (formerly Twitter Blue)
Subscription service offering verification, longer posts, and other features.

### Spaces
Audio conversation feature similar to Clubhouse.

### Communities
Topic-based groups for discussions.

## Changes Under Musk

Significant changes after Musk's acquisition:
- Mass layoffs (approximately 80% of staff)
- Verification changes (paid verification)
- Content moderation policy changes
- Rebranding from Twitter to X
- API pricing changes

## Competition

X competes with Meta's Threads, Mastodon, Bluesky, and traditional social networks like Facebook and Instagram.

## Historical Significance

Twitter has been influential in political movements, news distribution, and popular culture, with phenomena like hashtags becoming part of mainstream communication.
