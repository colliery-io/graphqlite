# Shopify Inc.

Shopify Inc. is a Canadian multinational e-commerce company headquartered in Ottawa, Ontario. Shopify provides a platform for online stores and retail point-of-sale systems.

## History

Shopify was founded in 2006 by Tobias Lütke, Daniel Weinand, and Scott Lake. Lütke, a computer programmer, initially wanted to sell snowboards online but was dissatisfied with existing e-commerce products, so he built his own.

## Leadership

- Tobias Lütke, Chief Executive Officer and co-founder
- Harley Finkelstein, President
- Jeff Hoffmeister, Chief Financial Officer

## Platform

Shopify provides a complete e-commerce platform that allows merchants to:

- Build online stores
- Process payments (Shopify Payments)
- Manage inventory
- Fulfill orders (Shopify Fulfillment Network)
- Sell across multiple channels (social media, marketplaces)

### Products
- Shopify (core e-commerce platform)
- Shopify Plus (enterprise solution)
- Shopify POS (point of sale)
- Shop Pay (accelerated checkout)
- Shopify Markets (international commerce)

## Ecosystem

Shopify has a large ecosystem of:

- Third-party apps (App Store)
- Themes (template marketplace)
- Partners (agencies and developers)

## Competition

Shopify competes with:

- BigCommerce
- WooCommerce (WordPress)
- Squarespace
- Wix
- Adobe Commerce (Magento)
- Amazon (as a marketplace alternative)

## Acquisitions

Notable acquisitions include:

- Deliverr (2022) for $2.1 billion (fulfillment)
- 6 River Systems (2019) for $450 million (warehouse robotics)

## Merchants

Shopify powers over 2 million businesses in 175 countries, including major brands like Allbirds, Gymshark, and Heinz.
