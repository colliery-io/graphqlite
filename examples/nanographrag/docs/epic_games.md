# Epic Games

Epic Games, Inc. is an American video game and software development company headquartered in Cary, North Carolina. The company is known for Fortnite, Unreal Engine, and the Epic Games Store.

## History

Epic Games was founded in 1991 by Tim Sweeney in Potomac, Maryland. The company was originally called Potomac Computer Systems before being renamed to Epic MegaGames and later Epic Games.

## Leadership

- Tim Sweeney, Chief Executive Officer and founder
- Kim Libreri, Chief Technology Officer

## Major Products

### Fortnite
Launched in 2017, Fortnite became a cultural phenomenon with its free-to-play battle royale mode. The game generates billions in revenue through cosmetic purchases.

### Unreal Engine
Epic's game engine powers thousands of games and is increasingly used in film, television, and other industries. Unreal Engine 5, released in 2022, features advanced graphics technologies.

### Epic Games Store
A digital game storefront launched in 2018 to compete with Valve's Steam. Epic offers developers an 88% revenue share compared to Steam's 70%.

### Other Games
- Gears of War (sold to Microsoft)
- Unreal Tournament
- Infinity Blade

## Ownership

Epic Games is privately held. Major shareholders include Tim Sweeney (controlling stake), Tencent (40%), and Sony (small stake).

## Apple Legal Battle

Epic sued Apple in 2020 over App Store policies after Fortnite was removed for implementing direct payments. The case resulted in some changes to App Store policies but largely favored Apple.

## Competition

Epic competes with Valve (Steam), Unity (game engine), and various game publishers and platform holders.
