# Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company

Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company, Limited (TSMC) is a Taiwanese multinational semiconductor contract manufacturing company headquartered in Hsinchu Science Park, Taiwan. TSMC is the world's largest dedicated independent semiconductor foundry.

## History

TSMC was founded in 1987 by Morris Chang with support from the Taiwanese government. Chang pioneered the pure-play foundry model, manufacturing chips designed by other companies rather than designing its own.

## Leadership

- C.C. Wei, Chief Executive Officer (since 2018)
- Mark Liu, Chairman
- Morris Chang, founder and former Chairman (retired 2018)
- Wendell Huang, Chief Financial Officer

## Manufacturing Capability

TSMC operates the world's most advanced semiconductor manufacturing facilities:

- 3nm process node (2022)
- 5nm process node (2020)
- 7nm process node (2018)

The company manufactures chips for the world's leading technology companies and is the sole manufacturer of Apple's iPhone and Mac processors.

## Major Customers

TSMC's customers include:

- Apple (largest customer, ~25% of revenue)
- NVIDIA
- AMD
- Qualcomm
- Broadcom
- MediaTek
- Intel (for some products)

## Global Expansion

TSMC is building manufacturing facilities outside Taiwan:

- Arizona, United States (under construction)
- Kumamoto, Japan (opened 2024)
- Dresden, Germany (announced)

## Competition

TSMC's main competitors are:

- Samsung Foundry (South Korea)
- Intel Foundry Services (United States)
- GlobalFoundries (United States)
- SMIC (China)

## Strategic Importance

TSMC manufactures over 90% of the world's most advanced chips, making it strategically critical to the global technology industry and a focus of U.S.-China technology competition.
