# Uber Technologies, Inc.

Uber Technologies, Inc. is an American multinational ride-hailing company headquartered in San Francisco, California. The company provides services including ride-hailing, food delivery (Uber Eats), package delivery, and freight transportation.

## History

Uber was founded in 2009 by Travis Kalanick and Garrett Camp. The service launched in San Francisco in 2010 as "UberCab" before shortening the name to Uber.

## Leadership

- Dara Khosrowshahi, Chief Executive Officer (since 2017)
- Travis Kalanick, co-founder and former CEO (departed 2017, resigned from board 2019)
- Garrett Camp, co-founder

## Services

### Ride-hailing
- UberX (standard rides)
- Uber Black (premium)
- Uber Pool/UberX Share (shared rides)
- Uber Comfort
- Uber Reserve

### Delivery
- Uber Eats (food delivery)
- Uber Connect (package delivery)
- Uber Direct (delivery for merchants)

### Freight
- Uber Freight (trucking logistics)

## Business Model

Uber operates as a platform connecting independent drivers with passengers. The company has faced regulatory challenges regarding worker classification, with debates over whether drivers should be classified as employees or independent contractors.

## Competition

Uber competes with:

- Lyft (ride-hailing, US)
- DoorDash and Grubhub (food delivery)
- Didi (China, global)
- Bolt (Europe)
- Grab (Southeast Asia)

## Acquisitions

Major acquisitions include:

- Postmates (2020) for $2.65 billion
- Drizly (2021) for $1.1 billion
- Transplace (Uber Freight, 2021) for $2.25 billion

## Former Ventures

Uber sold several businesses:

- Uber ATG (autonomous vehicles) to Aurora
- Jump (bike sharing)
- Uber Elevate (air taxi) to Joby Aviation
