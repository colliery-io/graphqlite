# Anthropic

Anthropic is an American artificial intelligence safety company headquartered in San Francisco, California. The company focuses on developing safe, beneficial AI systems and is known for its research on AI alignment and interpretability.

## History

Anthropic was founded in 2021 by Dario Amodei and Daniela Amodei, along with several other former OpenAI researchers. Dario Amodei previously served as Vice President of Research at OpenAI. The founders left OpenAI due to disagreements about the company's direction and safety practices.

## Leadership

- Dario Amodei, Chief Executive Officer and co-founder
- Daniela Amodei, President and co-founder
- Tom Brown, co-founder (GPT-3 lead author at OpenAI)
- Chris Olah, co-founder (known for neural network interpretability research)
- Jared Kaplan, co-founder (scaling laws researcher)

## Products

Anthropic developed Claude, a family of large language models designed with safety as a primary objective:

- Claude (2023)
- Claude 2 (2023)
- Claude 3 (2024) including Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus variants

Claude is available through the Anthropic API and powers various enterprise applications.

## Funding

Anthropic has raised substantial funding:

- Google invested $300 million in 2023
- Amazon invested $4 billion in 2023, making it the largest investor
- Spark Capital, Salesforce Ventures, and other VCs have also invested

## Research

Anthropic is known for pioneering Constitutional AI, a technique for training AI systems to be helpful, harmless, and honest using a set of principles. The company also publishes research on AI interpretability and mechanistic understanding of neural networks.

## Competition

Anthropic competes with OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Meta AI, and various AI startups in developing large language models and AI safety research.
