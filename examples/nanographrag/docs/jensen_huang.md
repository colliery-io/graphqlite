# Jensen Huang

Jen-Hsun "Jensen" Huang is a Taiwanese-American businessman and electrical engineer. He is the co-founder, president, and CEO of NVIDIA, the world's leading designer of graphics processing units (GPUs) and a dominant force in artificial intelligence computing.

## Early Life

Jensen Huang was born on February 17, 1963, in Tainan, Taiwan. He immigrated to the United States as a child and grew up in Oregon. He earned a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from Oregon State University and a master's degree in electrical engineering from Stanford University.

## Career

### Early Career
Before founding NVIDIA, Huang worked at LSI Logic and Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) as a chip designer.

### NVIDIA
Huang co-founded NVIDIA on January 25, 1993, along with Chris Malachowsky and Curtis Priem. He has served as president and CEO since the company's founding.

#### Key Achievements at NVIDIA
- Invented the GPU (graphics processing unit) in 1999
- Pioneered CUDA parallel computing platform
- Transformed NVIDIA from a graphics company to an AI computing leader
- Led the company to become one of the world's most valuable

### Leadership During AI Boom
Under Huang's leadership, NVIDIA capitalized on the AI revolution. The company's GPUs, particularly the A100 and H100 data center products, became essential infrastructure for training large language models like those from OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic.

## Awards and Recognition

Huang has received numerous awards including:

- IEEE Founder's Medal
- Dr. Morris Chang Exemplary Leadership Award
- Named to Time 100 most influential people

## Personal Style

Huang is known for his distinctive style, including his trademark leather jacket, and for delivering dynamic keynote presentations at NVIDIA's GTC conferences.

## Wealth

Huang's net worth has grown substantially with NVIDIA's stock appreciation, making him one of the wealthiest people in the technology industry.
