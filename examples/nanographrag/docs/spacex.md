# SpaceX

Space Exploration Technologies Corp., trading as SpaceX, is an American spacecraft manufacturer, launch service provider, and satellite communications company headquartered in Hawthorne, California.

## History

SpaceX was founded in 2002 by Elon Musk with the goal of reducing space transportation costs and enabling the colonization of Mars. The company achieved the first privately funded liquid-propellant rocket to reach orbit (Falcon 1) in 2008.

## Leadership

- Elon Musk, Chief Executive Officer and Chief Engineer
- Gwynne Shotwell, President and Chief Operating Officer
- Mark Juncosa, Vice President of Vehicle Engineering

## Launch Vehicles

SpaceX has developed several rocket systems:

### Falcon 9
The Falcon 9 is a partially reusable medium-lift launch vehicle that has become the workhorse of the company. It features first-stage landing and reuse, dramatically reducing launch costs.

### Falcon Heavy
The Falcon Heavy, first launched in 2018, is the most powerful operational rocket in the world by a factor of two.

### Starship
Starship is a fully reusable super heavy-lift launch system currently in development, intended for Mars colonization and other deep space missions.

## Spacecraft

- Dragon: Cargo and crew spacecraft for International Space Station missions
- Crew Dragon: Human-rated variant that carries NASA astronauts under the Commercial Crew Program

## Starlink

Starlink is SpaceX's satellite internet constellation, providing high-speed internet globally. The network consists of thousands of satellites in low Earth orbit and serves customers in over 60 countries.

## Competition

SpaceX competes with:

- Blue Origin (founded by Jeff Bezos)
- United Launch Alliance (Boeing and Lockheed Martin joint venture)
- Rocket Lab
- Arianespace
- China's government space program

## NASA Partnership

SpaceX works closely with NASA, providing cargo and crew transportation to the International Space Station and developing the Human Landing System for the Artemis program's lunar missions.
