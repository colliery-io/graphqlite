# Apple Inc.

Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California. The company was founded on April 1, 1976, by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in Los Altos, California.

## Leadership

Tim Cook has served as Chief Executive Officer since August 2011, following the resignation of Steve Jobs due to health issues. Jobs passed away in October 2011. Other key executives include:

- Craig Federighi, Senior Vice President of Software Engineering
- Johny Srouji, Senior Vice President of Hardware Technologies
- Eddy Cue, Senior Vice President of Services
- Katherine Adams, Senior Vice President and General Counsel

## Products and Services

Apple designs, manufactures, and markets smartphones (iPhone), personal computers (Mac), tablets (iPad), wearables (Apple Watch, AirPods), and accessories. The company also operates various software platforms and services including iOS, macOS, iCloud, Apple Music, Apple TV+, and the App Store.

## Facilities

Apple Park, the company's corporate headquarters, opened in April 2017 in Cupertino. The campus was designed by Foster + Partners and features a circular main building nicknamed "the spaceship." Apple also operates retail stores worldwide and maintains research facilities in multiple countries.

## Competition

Apple competes primarily with Samsung, Google, and Microsoft in consumer electronics. In smartphones, Samsung and Xiaomi are major competitors. In personal computing, Microsoft Windows remains the dominant operating system. Apple's services compete with Spotify, Netflix, and Amazon Prime.

## Financial Performance

Apple became the first publicly traded U.S. company to reach a $1 trillion market capitalization in August 2018, and later became the first to reach $3 trillion in January 2022. The company is listed on the NASDAQ stock exchange.
