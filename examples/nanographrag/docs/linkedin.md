# LinkedIn

LinkedIn is an American professional networking platform owned by Microsoft. The company is headquartered in Sunnyvale, California, and operates the world's largest professional network.

## History

LinkedIn was founded on December 14, 2002, by Reid Hoffman, Allen Blue, Konstantin Guericke, Eric Ly, and Jean-Luc Vaillant. The platform launched on May 5, 2003. Microsoft acquired LinkedIn in December 2016 for $26.2 billion.

## Leadership

- Ryan Roslansky, Chief Executive Officer (since 2020)
- Jeff Weiner, former CEO (2008-2020), now Executive Chairman
- Reid Hoffman, co-founder and Board member

## Platform Features

### Professional Profiles
Resume-like profiles showcasing work experience, skills, and education.

### News Feed
Professional content sharing and engagement.

### LinkedIn Learning
Online courses and professional development (formerly Lynda.com).

### Job Listings
Job search and recruitment platform.

### Sales Navigator
Sales prospecting and lead generation tool.

### LinkedIn Premium
Subscription tiers offering enhanced features for job seekers, recruiters, and sales professionals.

## Microsoft Integration

LinkedIn has been integrated with Microsoft products including Microsoft 365, Dynamics, and Outlook.

## User Base

LinkedIn has over 900 million members in more than 200 countries, making it the dominant professional networking platform globally.

## Competition

LinkedIn faces limited direct competition in professional networking, though platforms like Glassdoor, Indeed, and newer professional communities compete in specific areas.

## Revenue

LinkedIn generates revenue from talent solutions (recruiting), marketing solutions (advertising), and premium subscriptions.
