# SoftBank Group Corp.

SoftBank Group Corp. is a Japanese multinational conglomerate holding company headquartered in Tokyo. The company is known for its massive technology investments through the Vision Fund.

## History

Founded in 1981 by Masayoshi Son. Originally a software distribution company, SoftBank evolved into a telecommunications and investment conglomerate.

## Leadership

- Masayoshi Son, Founder, Chairman, and CEO
- Marcelo Claure, former COO (departed 2022)

## Major Holdings

### Telecommunications
- SoftBank Corp. (Japanese telecom)
- Sprint (merged with T-Mobile in 2020)

### Technology Investments
- Alibaba (early investor, largest shareholder until recent sales)
- ARM Holdings (acquired 2016 for $32 billion)
- Yahoo Japan

## Vision Fund

### Vision Fund 1 (2017)
- $100 billion fund, largest technology investment fund ever
- Major investors: Saudi Arabia's PIF, Apple, Qualcomm
- Notable investments: WeWork, Uber, DoorDash, Slack

### Vision Fund 2 (2019)
- Initially $108 billion target
- Primarily funded by SoftBank itself

## Controversies

The WeWork investment became a cautionary tale after the company's failed IPO in 2019 resulted in massive losses for the Vision Fund.

## Investment Philosophy

Masayoshi Son is known for his 300-year vision and willingness to make large, concentrated bets on transformative technologies.
