# Netflix, Inc.

Netflix, Inc. is an American subscription video on-demand over-the-top streaming service and production company headquartered in Los Gatos, California. Netflix pioneered the streaming video model and transformed the entertainment industry.

## History

Netflix was founded on August 29, 1997, in Scotts Valley, California, by Reed Hastings and Marc Randolph. The company initially offered DVD rentals by mail before launching its streaming service in 2007.

## Leadership

- Greg Peters, Co-Chief Executive Officer
- Ted Sarandos, Co-Chief Executive Officer and Chief Content Officer
- Spencer Neumann, Chief Financial Officer

Reed Hastings transitioned from CEO to Executive Chairman in 2023.

## Streaming Service

Netflix streams licensed and original content to over 230 million subscribers in 190+ countries. The platform offers movies, TV series, documentaries, and stand-up comedy specials.

## Original Content

Netflix produces original programming through Netflix Studios, including:

- Stranger Things
- The Crown
- Squid Game
- Bridgerton
- Wednesday

The company invests billions annually in content production across multiple languages and markets.

## Technology

Netflix runs on Amazon Web Services (AWS) and is known for pioneering microservices architecture and chaos engineering practices. The company developed the Chaos Monkey tool to test system resilience.

## Competition

Netflix competes with:

- Disney+ (The Walt Disney Company)
- Amazon Prime Video
- HBO Max (Warner Bros. Discovery)
- Apple TV+ (Apple)
- Hulu
- Paramount+
- Peacock (NBCUniversal)

## Business Model

Netflix operates on a subscription-based model with multiple tier options. The company introduced an ad-supported tier in 2022 to attract price-sensitive customers and has cracked down on password sharing.

## Gaming

Netflix has expanded into mobile gaming, acquiring game studios including Night School Studio and Boss Fight Entertainment.
