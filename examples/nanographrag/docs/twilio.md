# Twilio Inc.

Twilio is an American cloud communications company headquartered in San Francisco. The company provides programmable communication tools via APIs.

## History

Founded in 2008 by Jeff Lawson, Evan Cooke, and John Wolthuis. Lawson previously worked at Amazon and StubHub.

## Leadership

- Khozema Shipchandler, CEO (since 2024)
- Jeff Lawson, Co-founder and Board member (stepped down as CEO 2024)

## Products

### Communications APIs
- Programmable Voice
- Programmable Messaging (SMS, MMS, WhatsApp)
- Video
- Email (SendGrid)

### Twilio Flex
Cloud contact center platform.

### Twilio Segment
Customer data platform (acquired 2020 for $3.2 billion).

### Verify
Phone verification and two-factor authentication.

### TaskRouter
Intelligent task routing for contact centers.

## Business Model

Usage-based pricing where customers pay per API call, message, or minute.

## Key Customers

Twilio powers communications for companies like Uber, Airbnb, Netflix, and thousands of others.

## Acquisitions

Notable acquisitions include SendGrid (2019, $2 billion) and Segment (2020, $3.2 billion).

## Developer Focus

Twilio built its business on developer experience, with extensive documentation and developer evangelism.

## Challenges

In 2022-2023, Twilio faced growth challenges and announced layoffs, leading to strategic refocusing and CEO transition.
