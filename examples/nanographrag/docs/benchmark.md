# Benchmark Capital

Benchmark Capital is an American venture capital firm based in San Francisco. Known for its equal partnership structure and focus on early-stage investments.

## History

Founded in 1995 by Andy Rachleff, Bruce Dunlevie, Kevin Harvey, and Bob Kagle. The firm gained fame with its early investment in eBay.

## Leadership

Benchmark operates as an equal partnership with no senior/junior distinctions:
- Bill Gurley (known for Uber investment)
- Matt Cohler
- Peter Fenton
- Sarah Tavel
- Eric Vishria
- Chetan Puttagunta

## Notable Investments

### Legendary Returns
- eBay (1997) - returned over 100x
- Uber (2011) - Bill Gurley led
- Twitter
- Snapchat
- Instagram
- WeWork
- Discord
- Elastic

## Investment Philosophy

Benchmark is known for:
- Small fund sizes (typically $425-500 million)
- Equal partnership structure
- Focus on early-stage (Series A/B)
- High partner involvement
- Limited partners per fund

## eBay Success

Benchmark's $6.7 million investment in eBay in 1997 returned over $5 billion, establishing the firm's reputation.

## Bill Gurley

Bill Gurley is particularly well-known for his Uber investment and his blog "Above the Crowd" analyzing technology businesses.
