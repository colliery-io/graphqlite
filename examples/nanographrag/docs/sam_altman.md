# Sam Altman

Samuel Harris Altman is an American entrepreneur, investor, and programmer. He is the CEO of OpenAI, the artificial intelligence research organization behind ChatGPT and GPT-4.

## Early Life

Sam Altman was born on April 22, 1985, in Chicago, Illinois, and grew up in St. Louis, Missouri. He studied computer science at Stanford University but dropped out after two years to work on his startup.

## Career

### Loopt
In 2005, Altman co-founded Loopt, a location-based social networking mobile application. The company was acquired by Green Dot Corporation in 2012 for $43.4 million.

### Y Combinator
Altman joined Y Combinator as a part-time partner in 2011 and became president in 2014, succeeding Paul Graham. Under his leadership, Y Combinator expanded significantly and funded companies like Airbnb, Dropbox, Stripe, Reddit, and DoorDash. He stepped down from Y Combinator in 2019.

### OpenAI
Altman became CEO of OpenAI in 2019 after serving as a board member since the organization's founding in 2015. Under his leadership, OpenAI launched:

- GPT-3 (2020)
- ChatGPT (November 2022)
- GPT-4 (March 2023)
- Various other products and APIs

#### November 2023 Incident
In November 2023, the OpenAI board briefly fired Altman over "lack of candor." The decision was reversed within days following pressure from investors (particularly Microsoft) and a threatened mass resignation by OpenAI employees. Altman was reinstated as CEO with a new board.

## Investments

Altman has invested in numerous technology companies including:

- Stripe
- Reddit
- Instacart
- Retro Biosciences (longevity research)
- Helion Energy (nuclear fusion)
- Worldcoin (cryptocurrency/identity project he co-founded)

## Philosophy

Altman has written extensively about technology, startups, and the potential impact of artificial general intelligence (AGI) on society.
