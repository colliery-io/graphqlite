# Activision Blizzard

Activision Blizzard, Inc. was an American video game holding company that became a subsidiary of Microsoft following its acquisition in 2023. The company was one of the largest video game publishers in the world.

## History

Activision Blizzard was formed in 2008 through the merger of Activision, Inc. and Vivendi Games (which included Blizzard Entertainment). Activision itself was founded in 1979 as one of the first third-party video game developers.

## Microsoft Acquisition

Microsoft acquired Activision Blizzard in October 2023 for approximately $68.7 billion, the largest acquisition in video game industry history. The deal faced regulatory scrutiny in the US, UK, and EU before approval.

## Leadership (Pre-Acquisition)

- Bobby Kotick, former Chief Executive Officer (departed post-acquisition)
- Daniel Alegre, former President and Chief Operating Officer

Post-acquisition, the company operates under Microsoft Gaming, led by Phil Spencer.

## Studios

The company included numerous studios: Infinity Ward, Treyarch, Sledgehammer Games, Raven Software, Blizzard Entertainment, and King Digital Entertainment.

## Competition

Activision Blizzard competed with Electronic Arts, Take-Two Interactive, Sony Interactive Entertainment, and Nintendo in the gaming industry.
