# Qualcomm Incorporated

Qualcomm Incorporated is an American multinational corporation headquartered in San Diego, California. The company creates intellectual property, software, and semiconductors related to wireless technology.

## History

Qualcomm was founded on July 1, 1985, by Irwin Jacobs, Andrew Viterbi, and five other co-founders. The company name is a portmanteau of "quality communications."

## Leadership

- Cristiano Amon, President and Chief Executive Officer (since 2021)
- Irwin Jacobs, co-founder and former CEO
- Paul Jacobs, former CEO and executive chairman (Irwin's son)

## Products

### Processors
- Snapdragon mobile processors (used in most Android smartphones)
- Snapdragon X Elite (laptop processors competing with Apple Silicon)
- Automotive processors

### Modems
- 5G modems
- LTE modems
- Used by most smartphone manufacturers including Apple (historically)

### Other
- Bluetooth and Wi-Fi chips
- IoT processors
- XR (extended reality) platforms

## Licensing Business

Qualcomm's licensing business generates significant revenue from patents essential to 3G, 4G, and 5G wireless standards. This business model has led to legal disputes with Apple, regulators, and others.

## Customers

Major customers include:

- Samsung
- Xiaomi
- Oppo
- Vivo
- Microsoft
- Apple (for modems in certain periods)

## Competition

Qualcomm competes with:

- MediaTek (mobile processors)
- Apple (custom silicon)
- Samsung (Exynos processors)
- Intel (laptop processors)
- NVIDIA (automotive/AI)

## Manufacturing

As a fabless semiconductor company, Qualcomm designs chips but outsources manufacturing to foundries like TSMC and Samsung.
