# Figma, Inc.

Figma is an American collaborative interface design tool. The company pioneered browser-based design software with real-time collaboration.

## History

Founded in 2012 by Dylan Field and Evan Wallace. Field dropped out of Brown University after receiving a Thiel Fellowship. Figma launched publicly in 2016.

## Leadership

- Dylan Field, Co-founder and CEO
- Evan Wallace, Co-founder and CTO

## Products

### Figma
- Browser-based interface design tool
- Real-time collaboration
- Vector editing
- Prototyping
- Design systems

### FigJam
Collaborative whiteboard for brainstorming and planning.

### Dev Mode
Developer handoff features for translating designs to code.

## Technology

### WebGL-Based
Figma runs entirely in the browser using WebGL for rendering, unlike traditional native design applications.

### Multiplayer
Real-time collaboration with multiple designers working simultaneously.

## Adobe Acquisition (Terminated)

Adobe announced plans to acquire Figma for $20 billion in 2022, but the deal was terminated in 2023 due to regulatory concerns.

## Competition

Figma competes with Adobe XD, Sketch, and other design tools. Its collaborative features and free tier have driven rapid adoption.

## Community

Figma has built a strong community with:
- Community file sharing
- Plugin ecosystem
- Config conference
