# Blue Origin

Blue Origin, LLC is an American privately funded aerospace manufacturer and sub-orbital spaceflight services company headquartered in Kent, Washington.

## History

Blue Origin was founded in 2000 by Jeff Bezos, the founder of Amazon. The company operates with the motto "Gradatim Ferociter" (Latin for "Step by Step, Ferociously"), reflecting its methodical approach to space development.

## Leadership

- Jeff Bezos, Founder and Executive Chairman
- Dave Limp, Chief Executive Officer (since 2023)

Former CEOs include Bob Smith (2017-2023) and Rob Meyerson.

## Launch Vehicles

### New Shepard
A reusable suborbital rocket system designed for space tourism. Named after astronaut Alan Shepard, the first American in space. New Shepard has successfully carried passengers on suborbital flights to the edge of space.

### New Glenn
An orbital-class heavy-lift launch vehicle currently in development. Named after astronaut John Glenn. New Glenn is designed to be a reusable rocket competing with SpaceX's Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy.

## Rocket Engines

Blue Origin develops the BE-4 rocket engine, a liquid oxygen/liquid methane engine that will power both New Glenn and United Launch Alliance's Vulcan Centaur rocket.

## Space Tourism

Blue Origin offers suborbital space tourism flights on New Shepard. Notable passengers have included Jeff Bezos, his brother Mark Bezos, and actor William Shatner.

## NASA Contracts

Blue Origin, as part of the National Team (with Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman, and Draper), competed for NASA's Human Landing System contract for the Artemis program but lost to SpaceX. Blue Origin later received a separate NASA contract for lunar lander development.

## Competition

Blue Origin competes primarily with SpaceX in commercial space launch services and space tourism, and with Virgin Galactic in suborbital tourism.

## Facilities

- Headquarters and manufacturing: Kent, Washington
- Launch Site One: West Texas (New Shepard launches)
- Cape Canaveral, Florida (future New Glenn launches)
