# Meta Platforms, Inc.

Meta Platforms, Inc., formerly known as Facebook, Inc., is an American multinational technology conglomerate headquartered in Menlo Park, California. The company was founded as Facebook in 2004 and rebranded to Meta in October 2021.

## History

Facebook was founded on February 4, 2004, by Mark Zuckerberg, along with fellow Harvard University students Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes. The platform launched initially for Harvard students before expanding to other universities and eventually the general public.

## Leadership

Mark Zuckerberg serves as Chairman and Chief Executive Officer. Other key executives include:

- Javier Olivan, Chief Operating Officer
- Susan Li, Chief Financial Officer
- Chris Cox, Chief Product Officer
- Andrew Bosworth, Chief Technology Officer

## Products and Services

Meta operates several major social media platforms:

- Facebook (social networking)
- Instagram (photo and video sharing, acquired 2012)
- WhatsApp (messaging, acquired 2014)
- Messenger (messaging)
- Threads (microblogging)

The company also develops virtual reality products through Reality Labs, including Meta Quest headsets (formerly Oculus).

## Artificial Intelligence

Meta AI, led by Chief AI Scientist Yann LeCun, develops artificial intelligence research and products. The team released LLaMA (Large Language Model Meta AI), an open-source large language model that competes with OpenAI's GPT models.

## Metaverse

The company's rebranding to Meta reflects its focus on building the metaverse, a concept of interconnected virtual worlds. Reality Labs develops VR and AR hardware and software for this vision.

## Competition

Meta competes with TikTok in short-form video, with Snap in messaging and AR, with Twitter/X in microblogging, and with Google and Amazon in digital advertising.
