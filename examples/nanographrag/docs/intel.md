# Intel Corporation

Intel Corporation is an American multinational corporation and technology company headquartered in Santa Clara, California. Intel is one of the world's largest semiconductor chip manufacturers and was the dominant maker of processors for personal computers for decades.

## History

Intel was founded on July 18, 1968, by Gordon Moore and Robert Noyce, along with Andrew Grove who joined shortly after. Gordon Moore is famous for Moore's Law, the observation that the number of transistors on integrated circuits doubles approximately every two years.

## Leadership

Pat Gelsinger has served as Chief Executive Officer since February 2021, returning to Intel after previously serving as the company's first Chief Technology Officer. Other key executives include:

- David Zinsner, Chief Financial Officer
- Michelle Johnston Holthaus, CEO of Intel Products
- Sandra Rivera, CEO of Intel Foundry

## Products

Intel's product portfolio includes:

### Processors
- Intel Core (consumer CPUs)
- Intel Xeon (server/data center CPUs)
- Intel Atom (low-power processors)

### Other Products
- Gaudi AI accelerators (from Habana Labs acquisition)
- Mobileye autonomous driving technology
- Intel Foundry Services (contract manufacturing)
- Altera FPGAs

## Manufacturing

Intel operates semiconductor fabrication facilities (fabs) in the United States, Ireland, and Israel. The company is investing over $100 billion in new manufacturing facilities in Ohio, Arizona, and other locations to compete with TSMC and Samsung in advanced chip manufacturing.

## Competition

Intel faces intense competition from:

- AMD in x86 processors
- NVIDIA in AI accelerators
- TSMC in chip manufacturing
- Apple Silicon and ARM-based processors in PCs
- Qualcomm in mobile processors

## Challenges

Intel has faced challenges in maintaining manufacturing leadership, with TSMC and Samsung advancing to smaller process nodes ahead of Intel's own roadmap.
