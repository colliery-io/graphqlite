# Tesla, Inc.

Tesla, Inc. is an American multinational automotive and clean energy company headquartered in Austin, Texas. Tesla designs, manufactures, and sells electric vehicles, battery energy storage systems, and solar products.

## History

Tesla was incorporated on July 1, 2003, by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning. Elon Musk joined as chairman of the board in 2004 after leading the Series A funding round, and became CEO in 2008. The company is named after inventor and electrical engineer Nikola Tesla.

## Leadership

Elon Musk serves as Chief Executive Officer. Other key executives include:

- Vaibhav Taneja, Chief Financial Officer
- Tom Zhu, Senior Vice President of Automotive
- Drew Baglino, Senior Vice President of Powertrain and Energy (departed 2024)

## Vehicles

Tesla manufactures several electric vehicle models:

- Model S (sedan, 2012)
- Model X (SUV, 2015)
- Model 3 (sedan, 2017)
- Model Y (crossover, 2020)
- Cybertruck (pickup, 2023)
- Tesla Semi (commercial truck, 2022)

## Manufacturing

Tesla operates several manufacturing facilities worldwide called Gigafactories:

- Fremont, California (original factory)
- Giga Nevada (batteries)
- Giga New York (solar)
- Giga Shanghai (vehicles)
- Giga Berlin (vehicles)
- Giga Texas (vehicles, headquarters)

## Energy Products

Beyond vehicles, Tesla produces:

- Powerwall (home battery)
- Powerpack and Megapack (utility-scale batteries)
- Solar Roof and solar panels

## Competition

Tesla competes with:

- Traditional automakers: Ford, General Motors, Volkswagen, BMW
- EV startups: Rivian, Lucid Motors, BYD
- Other EV divisions of established manufacturers

## Autonomous Driving

Tesla develops Full Self-Driving (FSD) software and Autopilot driver assistance features. The company designed its own AI training computer (Dojo) and uses custom AI chips designed by former Apple engineer Pete Bannon.
