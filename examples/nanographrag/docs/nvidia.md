# NVIDIA Corporation

NVIDIA Corporation is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Santa Clara, California. The company is the dominant designer of graphics processing units (GPUs) and has become central to artificial intelligence computing infrastructure.

## History

NVIDIA was founded on January 25, 1993, by Jensen Huang, Chris Malachowsky, and Curtis Priem. The company invented the GPU in 1999 with the GeForce 256, revolutionizing computer graphics and later enabling parallel computing and AI acceleration.

## Leadership

Jensen Huang has served as President and Chief Executive Officer since the company's founding. Other key executives include:

- Colette Kress, Chief Financial Officer
- Debora Shoquist, Executive Vice President of Operations
- Ajay Puri, Executive Vice President of Worldwide Field Operations

## Products

NVIDIA's product portfolio includes:

### Data Center GPUs
- H100 and H200 (Hopper architecture)
- A100 (Ampere architecture)
- Used extensively for AI training and inference

### Consumer GPUs
- GeForce RTX 40 series (gaming)
- GeForce RTX 30 series

### Other Products
- DGX AI supercomputers
- NVIDIA Drive (automotive AI)
- Jetson (edge AI computing)
- Omniverse (3D simulation)

## AI Dominance

NVIDIA GPUs power the vast majority of AI training infrastructure worldwide. Major customers include Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta, and OpenAI. The company's CUDA programming platform has become the standard for GPU computing.

## Competition

NVIDIA competes with AMD in GPUs and with Intel in data center processors. Emerging AI chip competitors include:

- AMD with Instinct accelerators
- Intel with Gaudi accelerators
- Google with TPUs
- Startups like Cerebras, Graphcore, and Groq

## Market Position

NVIDIA became one of the most valuable companies in the world during the AI boom, briefly reaching a market capitalization of over $3 trillion in 2024.
