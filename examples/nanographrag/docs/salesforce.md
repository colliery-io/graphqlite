# Salesforce, Inc.

Salesforce, Inc. is an American cloud-based software company headquartered in San Francisco, California. The company is best known for its customer relationship management (CRM) platform, which is the world's largest.

## History

Salesforce was founded on March 8, 1999, by Marc Benioff, Parker Harris, Dave Moellenhoff, and Frank Dominguez. The company pioneered the software-as-a-service (SaaS) model and helped establish cloud computing as a mainstream enterprise technology.

## Leadership

- Marc Benioff, Chair and Chief Executive Officer (founder)
- Brian Millham, President and Chief Operating Officer
- Amy Weaver, President and Chief Financial Officer

## Products

Salesforce offers a comprehensive suite of cloud services:

### CRM Platform
- Sales Cloud (sales automation)
- Service Cloud (customer service)
- Marketing Cloud (marketing automation)
- Commerce Cloud (e-commerce)

### Platform Services
- Salesforce Platform (app development)
- MuleSoft (integration)
- Tableau (data visualization)
- Slack (workplace communication)

### AI
- Einstein AI (embedded AI across products)
- Einstein GPT (generative AI features)

## Acquisitions

Major acquisitions include:

- Tableau (2019) for $15.7 billion
- Slack (2021) for $27.7 billion
- MuleSoft (2018) for $6.5 billion
- ExactTarget (2013) for $2.5 billion

## Competition

Salesforce competes with:

- Microsoft Dynamics 365
- Oracle CX Cloud
- SAP Customer Experience
- HubSpot
- Zoho CRM

## Salesforce Tower

Salesforce Tower is the company's headquarters building in San Francisco, completed in 2018. It is the tallest building in San Francisco at 1,070 feet.

## Philanthropy

Salesforce pioneered the 1-1-1 model of corporate philanthropy, pledging 1% of equity, 1% of product, and 1% of employee time to charitable causes.
